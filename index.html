Friendship is not just something you find. It’s something you practice.

The real problem

Closeness fades when friendship has no practice.

Moving, work, breakups, remote work, and busy seasons can quietly break your social rhythm. You can meet people, but the connection doesn’t always grow. A good conversation doesn’t always become a real plan. An acquaintance doesn’t always become a friend.

Advice is everywhere. But it is often vague. You can understand what’s missing, but still not know what to do next.

It can feel awkward to restart. That’s normal. You are not the only one who feels this way.

Friendship needs a little magic: timing, mutual interest, and a desire to deepen the connection. The Friendship Practice does not try to force that magic. It helps you care for it once it is there.

What I do

A practical way to keep the connection alive, without making friendship feel forced.

But when a connection exists, friendship often grows through small things: making room, remembering, inviting, listening, following up, showing up again.

This is not only a skill. It is also a way of being: presence, warmth, timing, attention, and the courage to let someone matter. That is where practice helps.

We notice the feeling.

We look at where connection feels natural, where it fades, where you hesitate, and what kind of friendship you actually want more of in your life.

We make the next move feel like you.

Friendship is not only what you do. It is also timing, tone, attention, courage, and trust. We find a next step that feels warm, respectful, and real.

We care for what could grow.

A coffee, a walk, a message, a call, a photo, a second invitation: small things can carry a friendship forward when they are done with care and consistency.

About

I believe that, at the end of a life, what stays with us is not the money we made, the clothes we bought, or the furniture we owned. What stays are the people: who we traveled with, who was there for the sunset, who we called, who showed up, and who made ordinary days feel alive.

Living between continents has taught me that friendship does not survive by accident. I have learned how to keep long-distance friendships alive, how to reconnect, and how to keep making new friends as an adult.

My approach is practical, not clinical, but it is not mechanical. We work on the small real-life practices that support friendship, and on the way of being behind them: presence, warmth, timing, courage, and care.

The practice package

Four private sessions. One real friendship practice after each session.

Each session starts from one real situation in your life: someone you want to reconnect with, a new person you would like to know better, a friendship that feels uneven, or a moment where you are not sure how to move closer without forcing it.

We look at what is happening, what the relationship can realistically hold, and what kind of next step would feel respectful, warm, and possible.

After each session, you leave with one small friendship practice to try in real life. Then we use the next session to look at what happened, what it opened, what felt hard, and what the relationship may need next.

By the end of the four sessions, you should be on your way to creating new friendships, deepening the ones you already have, or feeling more confident about making room for friendship in your life.

Sessions are online, 45 to 60 minutes.

Common questions

Is this therapy? +

No. The Friendship Practice is coaching, not therapy, mental health care, diagnosis, or crisis support. The work is practical: real-life friendship practices, clearer choices, and what you can try between sessions.

Will I make friends? +

No one can guarantee that another person will become your friend. Friendship always needs mutual interest, timing, and chemistry. What this work can do is help you notice what could grow, stop staying stuck in vague intention, and practice the small moves that keep connection alive.

Is this for women only? +

No. This program is for adults of any gender who want a more intentional friendship life. The work is especially useful if you know people but want more real closeness, consistency, or follow-through.

What happens after I pay? +
