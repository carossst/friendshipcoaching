What is friendship coaching?

Friendship coaching is practical support for adults who want help navigating the real challenges of adult social life: meeting people, following up, reconnecting, and keeping friendships alive. It is not therapy. It is not crisis support. It is a structured way to turn good intentions into action.

Friendship coaching is a form of practical coaching that focuses on one specific area of life: the relationships we call friendships. It helps adults identify what is getting in the way of the connections they want, and build the specific habits and actions that allow those connections to develop.

What friendship coaching actually involves

In a friendship coaching session, you look honestly at your current friendship life: who is in it, what feels frustrating, what you want more of, and what is in the way. Then you identify specific, real-life actions: who to reach out to, what to say, what context to invest in, what patterns to change.

After each session, you leave with one concrete friendship action to try before the next. Not homework in the abstract sense, but one specific move that fits your actual life.

The work is practical, forward-looking, and grounded in what is happening right now, not in theory or diagnosis.

Research on behavioral goal-setting consistently shows that people are significantly more likely to follow through on a social intention when it is translated into one specific, concrete action with a clear timeframe, rather than remaining at the level of general resolve. That principle is what friendship coaching is built on: not insight about what you should do, but clarity about what you will do before the next session.

What friendship coaching is not

Friendship coaching is not therapy. It does not treat mental health conditions, provide a diagnosis, address trauma, or offer crisis support. If you are struggling with depression, severe anxiety, or thoughts of harming yourself, please seek support from a qualified mental health professional.

It is also not a social skills training program, a script-writing service, or a promise that specific people will want to be your friend. No coach can guarantee that another person will reciprocate connection. What coaching can do is help you act more clearly and consistently on the connections that have real potential.

Who friendship coaching is for

Friendship coaching tends to be most useful for adults who are going through a transition: a move to a new city, a job change, remote work, new parenthood, a life phase where their existing friendships have faded, or a period of building connection after a long stretch of isolation.

It also helps people who have the social opportunities but struggle with the follow-through: they meet people they like, have good conversations, and then nothing happens. Or they have people in their lives they care about but have been meaning to reconnect with for months.

If you are curious whether this applies to you, the free 7-day Friendship Challenge is a practical starting point. It helps you see your friendship life clearly before deciding whether coaching is the right next step.

How The Friendship Practice works

The Friendship Practice is a private 4-session coaching package offered by Carole Stromboni. Sessions are 45 to 60 minutes and usually take place on Google Meet.

Each session focuses on one specific friendship situation: a reconnection you want to attempt, a new connection you want to deepen, a context you want to invest in, or a pattern you want to change. You leave with a concrete action step to try before the next session.

The entry point is the free 7-day Friendship Challenge. It is a 7-day reflection sequence, available as both a browser-based tracker and a daily email series, that helps you map your friendship life before trying to change it.

You do not need a more charismatic personality. You need a more deliberate practice.

Is friendship coaching right for you?

If you find yourself thinking about friendship often but not acting on those thoughts, or if you have been meaning to reach out, reconnect, or invest in a new connection and keep postponing it, coaching can help you close that gap.

The goal is not a social transformation. It is one clearer step, then another, until the friendship life you want starts to take actual shape. To understand why adult friendship is structurally harder than it used to be, start with why making friends as an adult is hard.

Common questions

Quick answers

What is friendship coaching? +

Friendship coaching is practical support for adults who want to make new friends, reconnect with old ones, or keep existing friendships alive. It focuses on specific actions, real-life situations, and follow-through, not on theory or diagnosis.

Is friendship coaching the same as therapy? +

No. Friendship coaching is practical coaching, not therapy, mental health care, or crisis support. It does not treat mental health conditions or provide diagnoses. If you are struggling with something beyond the practical, please seek support from a qualified mental health professional.

Who is friendship coaching for? +

Adults who want practical help making new friends, reconnecting with old ones, or deepening current friendships. It is particularly useful during life transitions: moving to a new city, starting remote work, rebuilding a social life after a difficult period.

Does friendship coaching guarantee that I will make friends? +

No. No coach can guarantee that specific people will want to be your friend. What coaching can do is help you act more clearly and consistently on the connections that have real potential, so that more of those connections actually develop.

How is The Friendship Practice different from a self-help book? +

A book gives you general information. Coaching applies that information to your specific situation: your people, your patterns, your real-life obstacles, and your next concrete step. The difference is between understanding something in theory and actually doing something about it.

How do I know if I am ready for friendship coaching? +

Start with the free 7-day Friendship Challenge. It helps you look clearly at your friendship life before deciding whether coaching is the right next step. If after the challenge you want support applying what you noticed to one real friendship situation, coaching is a natural next step.
