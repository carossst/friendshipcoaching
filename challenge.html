The 7 days

Day 1 Unlocked

Map your friendship life as it is today

Before trying to make new friends, start with what exists. Who is close? Who is present but not close? Who has drifted? Who is an acquaintance that could become more?

Draw concentric circles on paper. Put your closest people in the center, then friends, friendly connections, acquaintances, and people you have drifted from.

Day 2 Locked

Name what feels frustrating or painful

Friendship frustration is often vague until you name it. Maybe you initiate too much. Maybe you feel forgotten. Maybe conversations stay shallow.

List what bothers you in the friendships you have or the friendships you do not have. Keep it honest and specific.

Day 3 Locked

Define what friendship means to you

People use the word “friend” differently. For one person, friendship means regular contact. For another, it means depth, loyalty, humor, or shared history.

Write three characteristics of a real friendship for you.

Day 4 Locked

Notice your friendship manual

Most of us carry quiet rules about how friends should behave. Some expectations are important. Some are unspoken manuals that other people never agreed to follow.

Complete this sentence: “A real friend should...” Then ask: is this a need, a preference, a request, or an unspoken rule?

Day 5 Locked

See different friendship styles

Some people never initiate, but always show up when called. Some are fun but not emotionally deep. Some are steady but low-frequency.

Pick one person and identify their friendship style without judging it too fast.

Day 6 Locked

Decide what you accept and what you need

Friendship practice includes acceptance. But acceptance is not the same as abandoning what matters to you.

Choose one frustration. Ask: can I accept this person as they are, do I need to make a request, or is this not the friendship I want to keep chasing?

Day 7 Locked

Choose your friendship practice

You do not need a coach to send one text or suggest one coffee. The deeper work is knowing what you are practicing and why.

Look back at your map, frustrations, definition, expectations, and needs. Choose one area to practice: reaching out, receiving, following up, being honest, making room, accepting difference, or choosing better.
